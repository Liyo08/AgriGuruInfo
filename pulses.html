Pulses

Overview

Pulses are leguminous crops harvested for their dry seeds, providing an essential source of plant-based proteins, dietary fiber, and nutrients. They help in soil fertility through nitrogen fixation, making them a vital component of sustainable farming systems.

Details

Climate & Season: Grows best in moderate to warm climates with well-distributed rainfall.
Soil: Well-drained loamy or sandy loam soils.
Watering: Requires minimal to moderate irrigation based on rainfall.
Fertilizers: Often needs minimal nitrogen fertilizer due to nitrogen fixation; phosphorus and potash may be applied.
Challenges: Pest control, moisture management, and disease prevention are essential in pulse cultivation.

Detailed Crop Information

Chickpea (Gram)

Overview: Chickpea is a major rabi pulse, rich in protein, used in various cuisines.

Season: Rabi season, sown in October-November.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 100-120 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing using seed drills.

Watering: Requires 1-2 irrigations.

Pest Management: Monitor for pod borers and wilt.

Harvest: Harvest when plants turn yellow and dry.

Pigeon Pea (Arhar/Tur)

Overview: Pigeon pea is a perennial legume used as a pulse and fodder.

Season: Kharif season, sown in June-July.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 150-200 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing in rows.

Watering: Requires minimal irrigation.

Pest Management: Monitor for pod borers and wilt.

Harvest: Harvest when pods are dry and brown.

Green Gram (Moong)

Overview: Green gram is a short-duration pulse crop, rich in proteins.

Season: Grown in both Kharif and summer seasons.

Soil: Sandy loam to loam soils.

Growth Cycle: 60-90 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing.

Watering: Requires minimal irrigation.

Pest Management: Monitor for yellow mosaic virus and thrips.

Harvest: Harvest when pods turn black and dry.

Black Gram (Urad)

Overview: Black gram is a protein-rich pulse crop commonly grown in India.

Season: Kharif and summer seasons.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 70-90 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing in rows.

Watering: Requires 2-3 irrigations.

Pest Management: Monitor for leaf spots and aphids.

Harvest: Harvest when pods dry and turn black.

Lentil (Masoor)

Overview: Lentil is a winter season pulse, rich in protein and iron.

Season: Rabi season, sown in November.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 100-110 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing.

Watering: Requires minimal irrigation.

Pest Management: Monitor for rust and wilt.

Harvest: Harvest when pods turn brown and dry.

Field Pea (Matar)

Overview: Field peas are grown for green vegetables and dry seeds.

Season: Rabi season, sown in October-November.

Soil: Loamy soils with good drainage.

Growth Cycle: 90-120 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing.

Watering: Requires 2-3 irrigations.

Pest Management: Monitor for powdery mildew and aphids.

Harvest: Harvest when pods are full and mature.

Cowpea (Lobia)

Overview: Cowpea is a drought-tolerant pulse used for vegetables and dry seeds.

Season: Kharif and summer seasons.

Soil: Sandy loam soils.

Growth Cycle: 70-90 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing in rows.

Watering: Requires minimal watering.

Pest Management: Monitor for aphids and mosaic virus.

Harvest: Harvest when pods turn dry.

Horse Gram (Kulthi)

Overview: Horse gram is a hardy pulse crop grown in dryland conditions.

Season: Kharif season, sown in June-July.

Soil: Sandy loam to loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 100-120 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing.

Watering: Requires minimal irrigation.

Pest Management: Monitor for leaf spot and rust.

Harvest: Harvest when pods are dry and brown.

Broad Bean (Fava Bean)

Overview: Broad bean is a cool-season pulse rich in proteins and fiber.

Season: Winter season.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 120-150 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing.

Watering: Requires moderate irrigation.

Pest Management: Monitor for aphids and chocolate spot.

Harvest: Harvest when pods are fully developed.

Bengal Gram (Desi Chana)

Overview: Bengal gram is widely grown for its protein-rich seeds.

Season: Rabi season, sown in October-November.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 100-120 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing.

Watering: Requires 1-2 irrigations.

Pest Management: Monitor for pod borers and blight.

Harvest: Harvest when plants dry and pods mature.
