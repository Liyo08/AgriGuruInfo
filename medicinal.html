Medicinal & Aromatic Crops

Overview

Medicinal and aromatic crops are used for their health benefits, essential oils, and therapeutic properties. They play a significant role in traditional and modern medicine, as well as in the fragrance and cosmetics industries, providing a sustainable income for farmers.

Details

Climate & Season: Varies by crop; many thrive in warm, subtropical climates.
Soil: Well-drained, fertile soils with good organic matter.
Watering: Moderate watering is needed, avoiding waterlogging.
Fertilizers: Organic manure and limited chemical fertilizers enhance quality.
Challenges: Requires careful pest and disease management to maintain medicinal value.

Detailed Crop Information

Ashwagandha

Overview: Ashwagandha is a medicinal herb used in Ayurveda for stress relief and immunity boosting.

Season: Sown in June-July.

Soil: Sandy loam soils with good drainage.

Growth Cycle: 150-180 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing of seeds.

Watering: Minimal watering required.

Pest Management: Monitor for leaf spot and wilting.

Harvest: Roots harvested when plants mature and leaves dry.

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Overview: Tulsi is revered in Ayurveda for its medicinal and spiritual benefits.

Season: Sown in June-July.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: Ready for harvest in 90-100 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct seed sowing or nursery transplantation.

Watering: Regular light watering.

Pest Management: Monitor for aphids and leaf spots.

Harvest: Leaves harvested during flowering for maximum oil content.

Aloe Vera

Overview: Aloe Vera is known for its medicinal uses in skincare and digestion.

Season: Can be planted year-round.

Soil: Sandy loam with good drainage.

Growth Cycle: Harvesting starts after 8-10 months.

Seed Cycle: Propagated using suckers.

Watering: Requires minimal watering.

Pest Management: Watch for aphids and fungal infections.

Harvest: Harvest mature leaves as needed.

Mint

Overview: Mint is an aromatic herb used for essential oil extraction and culinary purposes.

Season: Planted during spring (February-March).

Soil: Rich, moist, well-drained soils.

Growth Cycle: Ready for harvest in 90-100 days.

Seed Cycle: Propagated through root suckers.

Watering: Requires regular watering.

Pest Management: Watch for leaf spot and mites.

Harvest: Harvest before flowering for best oil content.

Lemongrass

Overview: Lemongrass is an aromatic grass used for essential oils and herbal teas.

Season: Planted during monsoon season.

Soil: Well-drained sandy loam soils.

Growth Cycle: Ready for first harvest in 4-6 months.

Seed Cycle: Propagated using slips.

Watering: Requires moderate watering.

Pest Management: Monitor for leaf blight and grasshoppers.

Harvest: Harvest leaves for oil extraction every 3-4 months.

Senna

Overview: Senna is used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties.

Season: Sown in June-July.

Soil: Sandy loam with good drainage.

Growth Cycle: Harvest in 140-160 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct seed sowing.

Watering: Minimal watering required.

Pest Management: Monitor for leaf spot and caterpillars.

Harvest: Harvest leaves and pods when mature.

Coriander

Overview: Coriander is used for its aromatic leaves and seeds, having medicinal and culinary value.

Season: Sown in October-November.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 90-100 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct seed sowing.

Watering: Requires moderate irrigation.

Pest Management: Monitor for aphids and powdery mildew.

Harvest: Harvest leaves and seeds when ready.

Basil

Overview: Basil is an aromatic herb used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Season: Planted in spring and summer.

Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.

Growth Cycle: 60-90 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct sowing or transplanting.

Watering: Requires regular watering.

Pest Management: Monitor for aphids and fungal diseases.

Harvest: Harvest leaves before flowering for best quality.

Marigold

Overview: Marigold flowers are used for medicinal and ornamental purposes.

Season: Sown in June-July or October.

Soil: Well-drained sandy loam soils.

Growth Cycle: 70-90 days.

Seed Cycle: Direct seed sowing.

Watering: Requires moderate watering.

Pest Management: Monitor for aphids and fungal diseases.

Harvest: Harvest flowers regularly for continuous blooming.

Neem

Overview: Neem is known for its medicinal properties and pest-repellent qualities.

Season: Planted during monsoon.

Soil: Well-drained sandy soils.

Growth Cycle: Slow-growing, but leaves can be harvested yearly.

Seed Cycle: Propagated through seeds.

Watering: Requires minimal watering once established.

Pest Management: Neem itself is pest-resistant.

Harvest: Leaves and seeds are harvested for medicinal uses.
